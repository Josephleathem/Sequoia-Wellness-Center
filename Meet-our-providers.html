Sequoia Wellness Providers

Rita Kinstle, MA, LPCC-S

Licensed Professional Clinical Counselor

With over three decades of expertise in the mental health field, Rita Kinstle, MA, LPCC-S, is a seasoned therapist devoted to helping individuals navigate challenges such as trauma, PTSD, addiction, emotional struggles, and personal growth. A staunch advocate for evidence-based care, Rita holds certifications in EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing), Brainspotting, DBT (Dialectical Behavior Therapy), Internal Family Systems Therapy, and Cognitive Behavioral Therapy. Her extensive skill set underscores her commitment to trauma-informed care.

Rita excels in guiding clients through transformative healing journeys rooted in resilience and self-discovery. Whether addressing depression, anxiety, mood and personality disorders, PTSD, addiction, or codependency, she adopts a compassionate, client-centered approach. Her goal is to empower individuals to achieve meaningful and enduring change in their lives.

Rita's vast experience includes serving as an Independent Contractor with the Department of Defense, where she provided therapy to active military personnel, their families, and Gold Star Families. This work ignited a profound passion for supporting veterans and their loved ones, forming a cornerstone of her practice.

As a Licensed Professional Clinical Counselor with Supervisor designation, Rita is also dedicated to mentoring the next generation of clinicians, fostering their growth and promoting ethical practices. Beyond her work with clients, she remains deeply committed to ongoing professional development and staying at the forefront of advancements in trauma, addiction, and mental health care.

Cristina Khuth, MSW, LISW-S

Licensed Independent Social Worker

Cristina Khuth, MSW, LISW-S, is a highly skilled Ohio Licensed Independent Social Worker with a supervisor endorsement and over 15 years of experience in the social work profession. She holds an undergraduate degree in Psychology from Temple University (Philadelphia, PA) and a Master of Social Work from Widener University (Chester, PA). Six years ago, Cristina transitioned her focus to clinical counseling and psychotherapy after many years of serving vulnerable women, children, and families.

Cristina specializes in working with adults and youth facing mental health and emotional challenges, substance abuse and addiction, and family and relationship issues. She has a deep passion for supporting trauma-impacted individuals and is extensively trained in trauma-informed care, EMDR, and cognitive-based therapies, including Dialectical Behavioral Therapy. Additionally, Cristina is licensed in Indiana as a clinical social worker, enabling her to provide telehealth therapy services to Indiana residents.
